Land Use Classification and Planning of Khaptadchanna Rural Municipality

The Beginning

Khaptadchanna RM lies in Bajhang district, nestled within the mid-hills of Nepal. The region is rich in natural beauty but also faces the pressing issues of unmanaged settlements, shrinking forests, and increasing demand for agricultural land. Our objective was clear: to classify existing land use and prepare a plan that would guide sustainable development.

Walking the Land

Field visits were the most memorable part of this journey. Carrying GPS devices, notebooks, and cameras, our team walked through farmlands, forests, and villages. Each conversation with locals revealed how land had changed over the years ‚Äî fields converted into houses, forests cleared for grazing, and roads opening up new opportunities. These observations were not just data points, they were stories of livelihood and survival.

From Pixels to Patterns

Back in the lab, satellite images and cadastral datasets became our canvas. Using supervised classification techniques in GIS, we categorized the landscape into agriculture, forest, built-up, barren land, and water bodies. The process was meticulous ‚Äî training samples, accuracy assessment, and validation with ground truthing. Slowly, the patterns of land use emerged, painting a digital portrait of Khaptadchanna.

Findings that Matter

The results revealed that agriculture still dominates the land, but built-up areas are expanding at a rapid pace. Forest cover, though significant, shows signs of fragmentation. These findings highlighted the urgent need for planned settlements, conservation efforts, and improved land management policies.

Planning Ahead

Our recommendations focused on sustainable practices: zoning for settlements, conservation of forest areas, proper utilization of barren land, and protection of agricultural fields from haphazard construction. For the local government, these maps and plans are more than just documents ‚Äî they are decision-making tools.
