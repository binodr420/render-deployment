Building a WebGIS with Django & Leaflet

Posted 2025-08-02

When I first thought about creating a WebGIS Portal for Bijayanagar, I had a simple idea: to bring different layers of local information ‚Äî schools, health facilities, roads, tourism, and even parcel-level data ‚Äî into a single interactive platform. What started as an experiment soon grew into a fully functional GIS portal powered by Django and Leaflet.

The Idea

Bijayanagar is a growing rural municipality, and like many local governments in Nepal, it faces challenges in planning, taxation, and resource allocation. I wanted to create a platform that would not just show maps, but also allow people to interact with land parcels, calculate tax, and explore services like education, health, and tourism.

Designing the Portal

The structure of the portal was simple but powerful:

  • Sidebar menu for layers (schools, health, government offices, roads, tourism).
  • Search bar to query parcels by unique ID.
  • Custom base maps ‚Äî local tiles and ESRI World Imagery.
  • Parcel Tax Calculator to estimate tax dynamically.

Building with Django & Leaflet

The backend was built using Django, which handled routing, database queries, and APIs for spatial data. On the frontend, Leaflet.js made maps interactive and lightweight. I fetched data as GeoJSON and displayed it with popups containing parcel details, school names, and ward numbers.

A key feature was the Admin Site. It allowed administrators to manage parameters like weightage, base rate, and road type values, giving flexibility to update taxation logic without changing the code. This made the portal not only dynamic but also sustainable for long-term municipal use.

One of the most interesting parts was the Parcel Tax Calculator. It considered parcel area, road connectivity, and road type (highway, feeder, pitched, paved, unpaved). With different weights, the calculator estimated a fair tax ‚Äî bridging geospatial data with financial planning.

Challenges & Learning

Building this portal wasn‚Äôt easy. I had to work through:

  • Integrating cadastral data with web maps.
  • Hosting custom map tiles and fitting them to exact municipal boundaries.
  • Ensuring that the portal runs smoothly even on limited internet connections in rural areas.

But these challenges taught me how to design scalable, user-friendly, and practical GIS applications.
